My Work

Mental Health Advocacy and Promoting Awareness

I worked for the nonprofit MIASA. MIASA, or the Mental Illness Awareness and Support Association, is a nonprofit organization based in Malaysia that is committed to promoting mental health awareness and offering support to individuals affected by mental health issues.

I personally assisted the organization to help achieve its mission by working across diverse fronts, on some key initiatives including setting up Support Groups, hosting Mental Health Workshops and Training, assisting our Counseling Services, liasing with key organisations for Collaborative Advocacy and participating in several Public Awareness Campaigns. I am incredibly proud to have been part of such a positive movement and to help champion mental health awareness in Malaysia.

This subject in particular is very close to my heart as I myself suffered from incredibly poor mental health during the COVID-19 Pandemic. I want everybody to know that they are not alone and that together we can overcome the negative stigma associated with mental health and go on to do truly great things. I am always willing to share my story to serve as inspiration to fellow sufferers.

Miss Petite Global Borneo 2023

A brief overview of the competition and why it is important.

Female Empowerment

The Miss Petite Global pageant is significant for Malaysians as it advocates for women empowerment, focusing on petite women who may face additional challenges due to conventional beauty standards. By providing a platform for these women to showcase their talents, intelligence, and capabilities, the pageant helps redefine beauty, fosters self-confidence, and encourages equal opportunities.

Championing Education and Social Causes

The Miss Petite Global Organization, through its "GAPAI" project, emphasizes the importance of education and social causes for Malaysians. By providing educational assistance to young, marginalized children, they work towards building a brighter future for underprivileged individuals in Malaysia. This focus on education also resonates with Malaysians' emphasis on the importance of social responsibility and community development.

Boosting Malaysia's Presence in the International Pageant Scene

Miss Petite Global enhances Malaysia's presence in the global pageant scene, showcasing the nation's culture and talent. As Malaysian representatives participate and perform well in such international competitions, it helps bolster Malaysia's image on a global stage and highlights the unique qualities of the Malaysian people, their culture, and their extraordinary talents.

Overview

Miss Petite Global was founded in Malaysia in 2020 by Nickson Sim and Leo Raynold to promote women empowerment through social causes, education, and innovation, especially for petite women. The Organization, through its "GAPAI" project, aims to provide education assistance to young, marginalized children.

Equal Opportunities

  • By breaking stereotypes around beauty, Miss Petite Global creates opportunities and platforms for petite women, showcasing that they are also capable, intelligent and empowered.

  • Miss Petite Global 2023 will take place in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia. The competition will uphold the values of education, nurturing individual talents, creativity, innovation, and self-confidence. The eligibility criteria for contestants include age, marital status, citizenship, height, physical attractiveness, and communication skills.
